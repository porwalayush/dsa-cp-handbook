How to Recognize a Stack/Queue Problem

Spot the signal, reach for the right container

1

"Most recent" or "matching pairs"?

Reach for a stack. Balanced brackets, undo history, and back buttons all care about the most recently seen thing first.

2

"In the order they arrived"?

Reach for a queue. Task scheduling, print jobs, and BFS all process items in arrival order — first come, first served.

3

"Next greater / smaller element"?

A monotonic stack. Keep the stack sorted; pop everything the new element beats. Turns an O(n²) scan into O(n).

4

"Max/min in every window"?

A monotonic deque. Drop elements that can never win again from the back, and expired indices from the front.

5

Parsing expressions?

Stacks evaluate and convert expressions — infix to postfix, and postfix evaluation both lean on a value/operator stack.

6

Level-by-level traversal?

A queue drives BFS. Process one level, enqueue its children, repeat — the queue holds the current frontier.

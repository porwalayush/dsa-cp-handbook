How to Recognize a Linked List Problem

Spot the signal, reach for the right tool

1

"Find the middle" or "is there a cycle?"

Use two pointers — slow moves 1 step, fast moves 2. When fast hits the end, slow is at the middle. If they ever meet, there's a cycle.

2

"Reverse" or "reorder"

Keep three pointers (prev, curr, next) and flip links one at a time. Never let go of the next node before you've saved it.

3

Head might change?

Use a dummy node in front of the head. It removes the special case of inserting/deleting at the very start.

4

"Nth from the end"

Advance one pointer N steps first, then move both together. When the leader reaches the end, the follower is on the answer.

5

O(1) insert/delete anywhere?

A doubly linked list lets you splice a known node out in constant time — the backbone of an LRU cache.

6

"Merge sorted lists"

Walk both with a pointer each, always attaching the smaller head to your result tail. A dummy node holds the new chain together.

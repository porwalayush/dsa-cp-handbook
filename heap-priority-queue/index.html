How to Recognize a Heap Problem

Spot the signal, reach for the priority queue

1

"Kth largest / smallest"?

Keep a heap of size K. For the Kth largest, use a min-heap of size K — its top is your answer. O(n log k).

2

"Top K frequent / closest"?

Count or measure, then push into a size-K heap. Pop the worst whenever it overflows, and the heap holds your winners.

3

"Merge K sorted things"?

A min-heap holds one head from each list. Pop the smallest, push its successor — the classic K-way merge in O(N log k).

4

"Running / streaming median"?

Two heaps: a max-heap for the lower half, a min-heap for the upper half. Keep them balanced; the median sits at the tops.

5

"Always take the cheapest next"?

A priority queue is the engine of Dijkstra, Prim's MST, Huffman coding, and task scheduling — greedily pop the best option.

6

"Schedule / process by priority"?

Push jobs with a priority key; pop the highest priority. CPU scheduling, event simulation, and A* search all lean on this.
